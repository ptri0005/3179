Largest Earthquakes

From the visualisation, it can be seen that the largest earthquakes in the world happen rather sequentially, namely alongst the borders of large tectonic plates. Unfortunately for Chile, 2 of the world's top 5 most intense earthquakes occured there as a country that resides alongst the edge of the South American plate. Earthquakes cause devastation by not only destroying the foundation of buildings through waves of powerful tremors, but also through huge tsunami waves that cuts across the mainland laying waste in its wake. The Valdivia earthquake alone had left over 2,000,000 homeless after the destruction.

Largest Flood

Historically, it can be seen that floods are one of the deadliest natural disasters. Primarily occuring in China, certain flood events can claim hundreds of thousands of lives - or even millions such as the 1931 China floods which had the Yangtze - Huai River flooding hit major cities such as Wuhan, Nanjing and beyond. Flooding not only damages infrastructure, but also livestock and agriculture that ensures famine to cause hundreds of thousands of deaths afterwards.

Largest Storm/Hurricane

When a storm's maximum sustained winds reach 74 mph, it is called a hurricane. The Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale is a 1 to 5 rating, or category, based on a hurricane's maximum sustained winds. The higher the category, the greater the hurricane's potential for property damage. Typhoon Ida had costed up to 64.8 Billion Dollars in damage repairs, stretching from cuba all the way through USA up to Canada. The top 10 hurricanes with the highest speeds have all originated either from the West or Western Pacfic.

Natural Disaster Trends Overtime

Earthquake Magnitude and Frequency Overtime

The magnitude of earthquakes seem to be slightly decreasing overtime, whereas the frequency of earthquakes have experienced a drastic spike in the 1990s. This means that we are experiencing rather consistent earthquake intensities but at a much more frequenct rate.

Flood Deaths and Frequency Overtime

Flood deaths experience a drastic drop after the 1920s-1940s, which included the catastrophic major river floods in China that have caused millions of deaths. Since then, better methods of dealing with floods have been put in place such as flood barriers and dams that have significantly reduced deaths.

Storm Speed and Frequency Overtime

Overtime, Storm Speed has been steadily decreasing whereas frequency has been drastically increasing ever since the 1980s - 2000s. However, from just the statistics we cannot conclude that storms have been growing less intense overall but the downwards trend in mean storm speeds may be attributed to less intense storms being recorded from an improvement technology.
